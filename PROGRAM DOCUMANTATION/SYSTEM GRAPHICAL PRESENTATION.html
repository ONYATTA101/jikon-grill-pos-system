Restaurant Service Flow

A single order becomes coordinated work for the waiter, kitchen, bar, cashier, inventory, and owner reports.

Step 01

Open Order

Waiter or cashier selects table, takeaway, or delivery service.

Step 02

Add Items

Products, quantities, prices, and notes are collected into the order.

Step 03

Route Tickets

Food goes to Kitchen. Drinks go to Bar. Direct items remain on the bill.

Step 04

Prepare

Each station sees only its work and marks completed items ready.

Step 05

Serve

The waiter marks ready items served after delivery to the customer.

Step 06

Pay

Cashier records an approved payment method and reference.

Step 07

Record

Receipt, stock deduction, reports, and audit history are updated.

Kitchen branch

Kitchen staff can change only food tickets assigned to the Kitchen station.

Bar branch

Bartenders can change only drink tickets assigned to the Bar station.

Order item→Bar queue→Ready→Served

Safety, Accountability, And Recovery

The system protects business information before, during, and after each transaction.

Signed sessions

HTTP-only signed sessions identify the user and protect role-restricted pages and APIs.

Login throttling

Repeated failed attempts are temporarily blocked to slow password guessing.

Role permissions

Staff see and change only the work required by their responsibilities.

Approval controls

Discounts, refunds, and voids require authorized Owner or Manager action.

Audit history

Sensitive actions record the responsible user, description, entity, and time.

Payment protection

Paid bills are locked against duplicate payment, and card numbers are never stored.

Stock integrity

Sales deduct inventory; voids reverse the related stock movement.

Daily closing

Expected and actual cash are compared, with variance notes retained.

Backup and restore

Timestamped PostgreSQL archives provide a guarded recovery path.
